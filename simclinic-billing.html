👩🏽 Angela Brooks Medicare Advantage
HSH-0007 · DOB: 01/27/1979 · Age 45 · Dr. Walsh · Deductible fully met
📋 THE SCENARIO
Angela is a 45-year-old established patient presenting today for her diabetes follow-up. She also has a non-healing wound on her right foot for three weeks — the provider documented poor healing related to her Type 2 Diabetes. Provider performs active wound debridement (CPT 97597) today and documents both diagnoses. Angela pays her copay at checkout. Her deductible has been fully met.
⚠ WATCH FOR
Both diagnoses must be coded — the diabetes AND the wound. Code linkage is critical: the diabetes diagnosis supports why the wound is not healing and why debridement is medically necessary. Because debridement is a separately identifiable service from the E/M visit, modifier -25 is appended to the E/M code. Verify Medicare Advantage eligibility day-of-visit. Copay must be collected and posted in EMR — never place in petty cash.
🔢 ICD-10-CM Diagnoses
ICDE11.621 — T2D with foot ulcer, right
ICDL97.519 — Non-pressure chronic ulcer, right foot, unspecified
⚙️ CPT Procedures
CPT99213-25 — E/M established, low complexity + mod 25
CPT97597 — Debridement, open wound, first 20 cm²
Your Task Checklist
MOD 11Verify Medicare Advantage eligibility in EMR before encounter is coded
MOD 11Confirm Angela is an ESTABLISHED patient — check last visit date in EMR
MOD 11Assign both ICD-10-CM codes — T2D with foot ulcer AND chronic ulcer detail
MOD 11Assign CPT 99213-25 (established E/M + modifier 25 for debridement visit)
MOD 11Assign CPT 97597 for wound debridement — verify it is <20cm²
MOD 11Link both diagnoses to both procedure codes in the EMR billing section
MOD 11Collect copay — post in EMR, issue receipt
MOD 11Submit claim electronically via EMR billing section
💬 DISCUSSION QUESTION
Why is modifier -25 required when billing both an E/M visit (99213) AND the wound debridement (97597) on the same day? What would happen to the claim if you forgot the modifier?
👨🏿 Marcus Webb Medicare Part B
HSH-0041 · DOB: 03/14/1973 · Age 52 · Dr. Evans · Deductible NOT met
📋 THE SCENARIO
Marcus is a 52-year-old presenting for his first visit to this clinic. He has T2D and hypertension that are not well-controlled — his A1C is 9.2. The provider performs a detailed history, comprehensive exam, and moderate-complexity medical decision making. A referral to Endocrinology has been placed — prior authorization is required before he can be seen. Marcus has Medicare Part B — outpatient visit. Copay is $20.
⚠ WATCH FOR
Marcus is a NEW patient — this is his first visit to this clinic. You must use a new patient E/M code (99203 or 99204), NOT an established patient code (99213/99214). This is one of the most commonly missed distinctions on the CCMA exam. Medicare Part B covers outpatient office visits — confirm this is NOT a Part A service. Prior auth must be in place before the Endo referral appointment.
🔢 ICD-10-CM Diagnoses
ICDE11.65 — T2D with hyperglycemia (A1C 9.2)
ICDI10 — Essential (primary) hypertension
⚙️ CPT Procedures
CPT99204 — New patient E/M, moderate complexity MDM
Your Task Checklist
MOD 11Verify Medicare Part B eligibility in EMR — confirm outpatient coverage
MOD 11Confirm Marcus is a NEW patient — no visits in past 3 years to this provider/group
MOD 11Assign ICD-10-CM for both diagnoses — T2D with hyperglycemia AND HTN
MOD 11Assign NEW patient E/M code (99203 or 99204) — NOT 99213 or 99214
MOD 11Verify both diagnoses support the level of service — code linkage
MOD 11Document prior auth referral for Endocrinology — flag auth pending in EMR
MOD 11Collect $20 copay — post in EMR, issue receipt
MOD 11Submit claim electronically; generate patient statement after ERA posts
💬 DISCUSSION QUESTION
What would happen if you used CPT 99213 (established patient) instead of 99204 (new patient) for Marcus? How does this affect the claim — and the clinic's reimbursement? How does the "3-year rule" apply here?
👩🏻 Sofia Martinez Aetna Student Plan
HSH-0004 · DOB: 11/05/2002 · Age 22 · Dr. Nguyen · $10 copay · Deductible NOT met
📋 THE SCENARIO
Sofia is a 22-year-old presenting for her scheduled Depo-Provera injection — this is her third injection visit. The provider orders medroxyprogesterone acetate 150 mg IM. The MA administers the injection in the left deltoid. No adverse reactions. Sofia has Aetna Student Health Plan. This encounter requires two separate codes — one for the drug (HCPCS J1050 × 150 units) and one for the administration (CPT 96372). Both are required for the claim to be complete and reimbursed.
⚠ WATCH FOR
This encounter requires TWO codes — the HCPCS J code for the medication AND the CPT code for the injection administration. Posting only one code leaves the claim incomplete and reimbursement is lost. J1050 is billed per mg — Sofia received 150 mg, so Qty = 150. CPT 96372 covers the therapeutic IM injection (subsequent — this is her 3rd visit). Verify Aetna eligibility and confirm student plan is still active. Student plans are verified annually.
🔢 ICD-10-CM Diagnoses
ICDZ30.42 — Encounter for injectable contraceptive management
⚙️ CPT + HCPCS Procedures
HCPCSJ1050 — Medroxyprogesterone acetate, per mgQty: 150
CPT96372 — Therapeutic/prophylactic IM injectionQty: 1
Your Task Checklist
MOD 11Verify Aetna Student Health Plan eligibility — confirm plan is still active
MOD 11Identify the correct HCPCS J code: J1050 for medroxyprogesterone acetate
MOD 11Enter Qty 150 for J1050 (drug is billed per 1 mg; 150 mg administered = 150 units)
MOD 11Identify the CPT administration code: 96372 (therapeutic/prophylactic IM, subsequent)
MOD 11Enter BOTH J1050 and 96372 in the EMR — verify both appear on the claim preview
MOD 11Link ICD-10 Z30.42 to both procedure codes — diagnosis supports medical necessity
MOD 11Collect $10 copay — post in EMR, issue receipt
MOD 11Submit claim to Aetna via EMR billing section
💬 DISCUSSION QUESTION
Why are two separate codes required for Sofia's injection — one HCPCS (J1050) and one CPT (96372)? What would happen to reimbursement if you only submitted J1050 and left out CPT 96372? Why does the drug quantity matter?
👨🏽 Michael Turner Workers' Compensation
HSH-0006 · DOB: 07/09/1990 · Age 34 · Dr. Evans · WC Case #WC332211
📋 THE SCENARIO
Michael is a 34-year-old presenting for a follow-up related to a work-related injury (right wrist strain sustained on the job). He has an active Workers' Compensation claim. Do NOT bill his personal insurance — all charges go to the WC carrier only. The provider documents a follow-up E/M visit with moderate complexity. Michael does not owe a copay — WC patients are never asked to pay. Confirm adjuster name and WC claim number before submitting.
⚠ WATCH FOR
Workers' Compensation is billed to the WC carrier — never to the patient or their personal insurance. There is no patient responsibility. The WC case number and adjuster contact are required on the claim. Michael should never be asked to pay for WC-covered services. Time requirements for filing WC claims vary by state — check the carrier's guidelines.

👩🏾 Evelyn Carter Dual Eligible — Medicare + Medicaid
HSH-0016 · DOB: 03/17/1953 · Age 71 · Dr. Lee · Patient owes $0 — Bill Medicare FIRST
📋 THE SCENARIO
Evelyn is a 71-year-old with T2D and CHF presenting for a follow-up visit. She is dual eligible — she has both Medicare (primary) AND Medicaid (secondary). The correct billing order matters: bill Medicare first, then Medicaid picks up the remaining patient responsibility. Evelyn owes $0 out-of-pocket. The provider performs an established E/M visit, low complexity.
⚠ WATCH FOR
Billing order is critical for dual-eligible patients. Always bill Medicare FIRST. After Medicare processes the claim and posts the EOB/ERA, bill Medicaid as secondary for any remaining patient responsibility. Evelyn should never be charged a copay or coinsurance — Medicaid covers all cost-sharing. If you bill Medicaid first, the claim will be denied.
🔢 ICD-10-CM Diagnoses
ICDE11.9 — T2D without complications, follow-up
ICDI50.9 — Heart failure, unspecified (CHF)
⚙️ CPT Procedures
CPT99213 — Established E/M, low complexity
Your Task Checklist
MOD 11Verify BOTH Medicare and Medicaid eligibility in EMR
MOD 11Confirm billing sequence: Medicare PRIMARY, Medicaid SECONDARY
MOD 11Assign ICD-10-CM codes for T2D and CHF — both active conditions
MOD 11Assign CPT 99213 for established E/M visit, low complexity
MOD 11Do NOT collect copay — Evelyn owes $0; Medicaid covers all cost-sharing
MOD 11Submit claim to Medicare FIRST — wait for ERA before billing Medicaid
MOD 11After Medicare ERA posts, crossover bill to Medicaid for $0 patient balance
💬 DISCUSSION QUESTION
Why must Medicare always be billed first for a dual-eligible patient? What is a "crossover claim" and when does it occur? What happens to the claim if Medicaid is accidentally billed first?

💡 CCMA EXAM TIP — CLEAN CLAIM A "clean claim" has no demographic or insurance errors AND accurately links ICD-10-CM diagnosis codes to the appropriate CPT/HCPCS codes to support medical necessity. 💡 CCMA EXAM TIP — "DENIED ≠ RESUBMIT" Resubmitting a medically-denied claim generates a duplicate denial and burns through your timely filing window. The correct response to a medical necessity denial is to file an appeal with supporting clinical documentation. A rejected claim (kicked back before reaching the payer) can be corrected and resubmitted. Know the difference — it comes up on the CCMA exam.
